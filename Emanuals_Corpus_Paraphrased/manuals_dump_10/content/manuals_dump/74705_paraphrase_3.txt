# ERN INSTRUCTION MANUAL

## IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION

This instruction manual should be kept for future reference. If the appliance is sold or transferred to another owner, ensure that the new owner has access to this manual, as it contains essential information about the appliance's operation and safety features.

**Safety Precautions:**

- The appliance is heavy; handle it carefully during installation and when moving. 
- Ensure to unplug the appliance before performing any maintenance. 
- Avoid exposing the appliance to heat sources or direct sunlight.
- Follow the guidelines provided in this manual for proper functioning.

**Using the Refrigerator:**

Allow the appliance to rest for at least two hours before turning it on to ensure optimal performance. Child safety is paramount; keep children away from the appliance, particularly during operation. 

Always ensure the appliance is level for proper cooling and avoid prolonged exposure to sunlight.

**Defrosting and Cleaning:**

When defrosting, avoid using sharp tools to remove ice, as this can damage the internal components. Follow the recommended steps for safe defrosting. 

**Service and Maintenance:**

Repairs should only be performed by authorized personnel to avoid damage or malfunction. Contact Service Force for authorized service.

## OPERATION

**Adjusting the Thermostat:**

Familiarize yourself with the thermostat and its functions. Adjust settings based on factors such as ambient temperature and the type of food stored. 

**Food Storage Tips:**

- Do not refreeze defrosted cooked food; consume it within a few hours after thawing.
- Store food in airtight containers to maintain freshness and prevent cross-contamination.
  
**Normal Operation:**

It is common to hear sounds from the appliance during operation. This includes whirring from the cooling mechanism and clicking sounds from the thermostat. 

## MAINTENANCE AND CLEANING

Disconnect the appliance from the electrical supply before cleaning. Use a damp cloth with mild cleaners; avoid detergents, abrasives, or strong scents that could damage surfaces and leave odors. 

To replace the interior light bulb, first switch off the power, then remove the light cover and bulb as per the instructions.

## TROUBLESHOOTING

If issues arise, refer to the following symptoms:

- **Light Not Working:** Check if the bulb is blown or if the switch is malfunctioning.
- **Excessive Frost Buildup:** This may indicate improper temperature settings or door seal issues.

For issues beyond simple troubleshooting, contact Service Force for assistance.

**Installation Instructions**: 
**Warning**: If disposing of an old appliance with a locking mechanism, ensure it is rendered unusable to prevent trapping children inside. Follow the appropriate installation guidelines for positioning, ensuring compliance with the specified temperature ranges:
- SN: +10°C to 32°C
- N: +16°C to 32°C
- ST: +18°C to 38°C
- T: +18°C to 43°C

The appliance should be sited away from sunlight and heat sources. For built-in installation, refer to the specific guidelines provided.

**Electrical Safety**: 
**Warning**: This appliance must be earthed. Ensure that electrical connections are made correctly. Wires should be connected as follows:
- Earth (E) wire is typically colored green/yellow
- Neutral (N) is blue
- Live (L) is brown or red

Use only connections that conform to BSI362 ASTA standards. The appliance must meet E.E.C. directives regarding electrical safety.

**Built-in Installation**:
Follow these steps for installation:
1. Unscrew the necessary components as per the instructions.
2. Ensure proper clearances are maintained during installation.
3. Secure the appliance with provided screws and fittings.

Check that all parts fit correctly before finalizing the installation to ensure the appliance operates efficiently. Adjustments may be necessary to maintain clearance during operation.

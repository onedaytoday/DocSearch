### 2444MP3 DE,GB,FR & HR PM User Guide

#### Setup and Remote Battery Replacement

To replace the remote battery, use a CR2025 lithium battery. Ensure the polarity matches the specified orientation. For FM stations, if reception is poor or distorted, switch from STEREO to MONO. This applies to both AM and FM modes, which also utilize the built-in antenna for better signal quality. Ensure that the terminal connections, including the left and right channels, are correct while connecting headphones (not included) to avoid high sound levels that could damage hearing.

#### Connecting the Subwoofer

To connect the subwoofer, use the RCA outputs and connect to an AC230V power source. Ensure connection is secure to comply with warranty conditions. If necessary, turn off the device via the AC cord when not in use.

#### Clock and Timer Setup

While the unit is off, adjust the CLOCK by pressing the PROGRAM button and repeatedly selecting the hour and minutes. Use the POWER button to turn on the device. Tuner and timer settings can be adjusted similarly, ensuring proper ON/OFF functions.

#### Basic Operation

To power on the unit, press the POWER button, then select your desired mode (TUNER, CD, AUX) by pressing FUNC. Adjust the volume accordingly using the VOL controls, and mute/unmute with the MUTE button. Cycling through EQ settings can be achieved by pressing the EQ button multiple times.

#### Listening to the Radio

Power on using the POWER button, then select the TUNER band (FM/AM) using the TUNER/BAND button. Use TUNING + or TUNING - buttons for station selection. For better reception, switch between ST/MONO as needed.

#### CD Player Operation

To access the CD player, press the POWER and FUNC or PLAY/PAUSE buttons. Insert the CD with the label facing up. The device will show "NO DISC" if unloaded. Use the PLAY and PAUSE buttons accordingly for playback control, and navigate tracks using the SKIP buttons. Utilize the REPEAT function for single, all, or album playback, and use the RANDOM function for shuffle play.

#### MP3 Player Operation

For MP3 playback, follow similar steps as the CD player. Properly insert the file and navigate tracks using SKIP. The presence of CD-R/RW media may affect playback quality based on recording factors.

#### Care and Maintenance

Clean the unit regularly using a lint-free cloth. Avoid using harsh cleaners. For scratched discs, consider using a CD cleaner to maintain performance.

#### Troubleshooting Guide

In case of issues, reset the device by disconnecting power. Refer to the manual for specific symptoms relating to sound quality or reception problems. Ensure the proper modes are engaged and troubleshoot as needed. Adjust antenna positioning for better reception if necessary.

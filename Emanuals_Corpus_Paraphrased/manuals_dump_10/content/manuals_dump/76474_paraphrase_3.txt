### Installation Instructions
#### Safety Precautions:
When installing this exhaust system, ensure that you follow proper safety guidelines:
- Use jack stands when working under the truck.
- Block tires and wear safety glasses and gloves.
- Avoid working with hot pipes!

#### Suggested Tools:
- 15mm Socket Wrench
- WD-40
- Jack Stand
- Hacksaw

---

#### Installation Steps:

1. **Clearance Check:** Ensure a minimum clearance between all pipes and components such as rubber brake lines, tires, shocks, fuel lines, etc.
   
2. **Torque Clamps:** Torque clamps to specified ft-lbs.

3. **Removal of Stock Exhaust:**
   - Cut the stock exhaust just in front of the muffler after the weld.
   - Retain all rubber grommets on the hangers.
   - Apply WD-40 to facilitate the removal of the exhaust and hangers.

4. **Install Head Pipe:**
   - Connect the head pipe to the existing stock front pipe and secure it with Clamp F. Do not tighten yet.

5. **Install Muffler:**
   - Attach the muffler to the head pipe with the louvers facing the converter.
   - Use a jack stand to support the muffler.
   - Secure the muffler to the head pipe using Clamp G. Do not tighten yet.

6. **Install Band Clamp:**
   - Place Band Clamp H onto the muffler and insert the welded hanger into the rubber grommet.
   - Tighten the band clamp using hardware kit I.

7. **Install Over-Axle Tailpipe:**
   - Insert the over-axle tailpipe into the muffler and secure it with Clamp G. Do not tighten yet.

8. **Install Turnout Pipe:**
   - Connect the turnout pipe to the tailpipe and secure with Clamp G. Do not tighten yet.

9. **Position Exit Pipe:**
   - Adjust the exit pipe to the correct angle for your truck and insert the welded hanger into the grommet.

10. **Finish Installation:**
    - Attach the stainless steel tip and secure with a clamp.
    - Once everything is properly aligned, securely tighten all bolts and clamps.

#### Maintenance:
To clean the stainless steel tips, utilize a stainless steel cleaner and a Scotch-Brite pad.

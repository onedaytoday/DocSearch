### Introduction
This touch monitor is designed to maximize the performance of Touch products. The manual provides detailed information on its features and specifications. The monitor utilizes an active thin-film-transistor LCD that delivers high-quality performance, including HD graphics and an LED backlight that minimizes energy consumption compared to traditional CCFL backlighting. Additional features include on-screen display (OSD) adjustments. Users are advised to follow safety precautions outlined in this manual, which also includes chapters on installation and mounting.

---

### Unpacking
Upon unpacking the monitor, ensure the following items are included: 
- Touch monitor with protective sheet 
- Installation CD 
- VGA cable 
- DVI cable 
- USB cable 
- Mounting hardware

### Connector Interfaces
The monitor supports DVI and VGA inputs. Ensure that connections are securely tightened to optimize performance. The USB connection will facilitate touchscreen functionality. The monitor requires a 12V DC power supply to function correctly.

---

### Software
For additional driver and software details, visit: www.elotouch.com. Ensure the proper drivers are installed for your operating system, including Windows XP installations.

### Mounting Information
The OSD text can be rotated through the mounting bracket settings. The monitor supports VESA mounting with M4 screws, compliant with the VESA MIS-E specification.

---

### Power Management
To power the monitor on or off, use the OSD controls. The LED indicator will display various states: ON, OFF, and SLEEP, which can be managed to conserve energy. The monitor comes factory-calibrated and should properly interface with connected computers. The touchscreen should respond accurately to user inputs.

---

### OSD Functions
The OSD includes a graphical interface for adjusting settings such as brightness, clock phase, scaling, and aspect ratio. Users can select different presets and languages, and change settings like OSD timeout duration. All adjustments can be seen in real time through the OSD.

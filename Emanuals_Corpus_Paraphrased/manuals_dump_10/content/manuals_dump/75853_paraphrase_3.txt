#### OPERATION
To operate the appliance effectively, adjust the thermostat according to your needs. Keep in mind that factors such as ambient temperature and food load can affect cooling performance. Ensure settings are appropriate for the types of food being stored and check regularly.

To maintain optimal freshness, do not overload the cooling shelves, and ensure items are appropriately spaced for air circulation. Fresh food must be stored correctly, with specific attention to temperatures. Do not refreeze defrosted cooked food unless absolutely necessary. Follow the guidelines for reheating and reheating times for food safety. 

#### MAINTENANCE AND CLEANING
Always disconnect the appliance from the electricity supply before cleaning. Use a damp cloth with mild detergents; never use abrasive agents or strong cleaning products that may damage the interior surfaces. 

For light repairs, switch off the appliance and disconnect it from the power supply before replacing the bulb. Follow the instructions provided for light replacement carefully. 

#### DEFROSTING
Defrost the appliance when frost exceeds certain thickness levels. Turn the thermostat off and prepare to remove ice build-up. Place towels or trays to catch excess water. If frost problems persist, inspect the thermostat settings and ensure the appliance is functioning correctly.

#### TROUBLESHOOTING
If you experience issues with the appliance, such as a malfunctioning light or temperature inconsistencies, review the troubleshooting guide. Ensure the thermostat settings are correct and that food items are not obstructing airflow. If problems continue, contact an authorized service technician for assistance.

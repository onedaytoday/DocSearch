## Important Safety Instructions

**Warnings:**
To mitigate the risk of fire or electric shock, please follow these safety precautions:

1. Ensure proper installation and grounding according to ANSI/NFPA (USA) or CSA C22.1, Part I guidelines.
2. Allow adequate cooling time after initial use before connecting to power supply.
3. Avoid servicing unless specifically recommended, to reduce fire hazards and ensure safety.
4. Keep flammable materials away from the oven, including cloth items and plasticware.
5. Supervise children around the appliance; they should not interact with it unsupervised.

**Cautions:**
- Do not touch interior oven elements or surfaces until they have cooled down, as they can cause severe burns.
- Use a foam-type extinguisher to smother any grease fires.
- When cooking, be cautious of unopened containers as they may burst due to pressure buildup.

## Baking Instructions
Preheat your oven to the desired temperature, typically ranging from 170°F to 550°F, with 350°F being standard. For additional guidance on specific cooking temperatures and techniques, refer to the baking section.

---

For any additional general or troubleshooting questions, consult the common issues section or the warranty information.

### COOK TIME
COOK TIME enables the appliance to automatically manage cooking durations. The timer indicates the remaining time, and the system will turn off once the countdown is complete. This feature is compatible with various modes including Convection Warm, Defrost, Proof, and Perfect Cooking. To cook at 450°F, press the OVEN button, then select BAKE. Use COOK OPTIONS to explore different cooking settings. Press COOK TIME and then START to commence heating. You can press CANCEL to interrupt the cooking cycle at any time. The END TIME feature allows you to schedule when cooking will conclude, with the countdown visible. Ensure meats or poultry are cooked thoroughly to avoid foodborne illnesses, particularly if cooking exceeds one hour, and remove food promptly when done.

### SAFETY NOTE
In case of a fire, turn off the appliance and do not attempt to extinguish it with water. Use a fire extinguisher for grease fires to prevent injury.

### BROILING
Broiling utilizes high heat to cook meats thoroughly while enhancing their outer appearance. This mode operates between 300°F to 550°F. For optimal results, preheat the oven, season the food, and turn it as needed. Ensure grease does not ignite; do not cover the broiling food with foil. 

### CONVECTION BAKING
Convection Baking optimizes cooking by circulating heated air, enhancing texture. It operates between 170°F to 550°F, with an optimal setting around 350°F. Preheat as recommended to ensure even cooking, and reduce temperature if using an existing recipe that hasn't been adjusted for convection settings. Ensure good airflow around the food for even cooking.

### CONVECTION CONVERT
This feature adjusts cooking parameters for convection usage, reducing temperatures by 25°F compared to conventional methods. For guidance, refer to the Convection Convert section as needed. To activate, press the OVEN button, select CONVECTION BAKE, and adjust accordingly.

### RAPID PREHEAT
Rapid Preheat is designed for quick heating before cooking begins, effective for preparing various dishes. It operates between 170°F to 550°F. To utilize this feature, press the OVEN button, select BAKE, and activate RAPID PREHEAT.

### CONVECTION ROASTING
Convection Roasting combines continuous airflow for even cooking and browning, ranging from 170°F to 550°F, ideally at 350°F. Ensure the roasting pan is securely placed and does not overfill to allow heat circulation.

### KEEP WARM
This function maintains the temperature of cooked foods, typically between 150°F to 190°F (recommended at 170°F), allowing them to stay warm until serving. To activate, press the OVEN button followed by KEEP WARM.

### SLOW COOK
Designed for low-temperature cooking, similar to a Crock-Pot, this setting allows for thorough cooking without burning. Operate on either high (HI) or low (LO) settings, and use a thermometer to confirm food reaches safe internal temperatures.

### DEHYDRATING
This mode evenly heats food to dehydrate it, operating between 100°F to 225°F, generally at 120°F. Preheat as necessary and adjust settings based on food type. 

### DEFROSTING
The Defrost function gently thaws food items like cakes, controlled so that surrounding areas warm without cooking. To utilize, press the OVEN button, select DEFROST, and start the cycle. Always monitor food to prevent bacterial growth during defrosting.

### BREAD PROOFING

Activating the yeast during the bread proofing process. The optimal proofing temperature ranges from 85°F to 100°F, with 100°F being ideal. **Proofing Tips:** Preheat the oven to the lowest setting to warm the cavity. Allow adequate time for the temperature to stabilize. To initiate proofing, press the "OVEN" button to illuminate the cooking display. Then press "BREAD PROOF" and "START" to activate. You can cancel the process anytime. While proofing is active, press "OVEN" to turn on the cooking light.

### PERFECT TURKEY

This function precisely manages the cooking of turkey. Set the temperature from 170°F to 550°F, with a common cooking temperature of 325°F and a target internal temperature of 180°F. **Tips for Perfection:** Ensure the turkey is fully thawed (allow at least hours for thawing), preheat using the Perfect Turkey setting, and place the turkey securely on the roasting rack to catch grease. The roasting pan should fit properly to allow for even cooking. To use this feature, press "OVEN" to illuminate the cooking options, then select "PERFECT TURKEY" and press "START." Utilize the "PROBE" function for accurate internal temperature measurement. Cancel anytime if necessary.

### TEMPERATURE PROBE

The temperature probe allows for precise testing of internal food temperatures to ensure proper cooking. This function works with convection modes. The probe can be set between 130°F to 210°F, generally peaking at 170°F. **IMPORTANT:** Avoid cross-contaminating the probe with other foods when checking temperatures. For optimal results, ensure food is fully defrosted before inserting the probe, placing it in the thickest part. Position the probe toward the bone for meat, or the thickest part of poultry. To activate, press "OVEN" and follow with "COOK OPTIONS" for the desired temperature setting. 

### MULTI STAGE COOKING

The Multi-Stage feature allows for sequential cooking stages, with up to three distinct cooking settings including convection options. Start by pressing "OVEN" to illuminate the cooking options, and select "MULTI STAGE" to begin. Follow the prompts to activate various cooking functions. You can cancel any stage at any time.

### USER PREFERENCES

The Preferences section allows for customization of your cooking experience. It includes options for continuous use, hour format, and clock settings. To access Preferences, press "USER PREF," select your desired options, and press "START" to confirm changes. 

**Setting the Clock:** If the clock needs adjustment—whether due to a power interruption or to reset to default settings—follow the prompts under "USER PREF" to access the clock options. 

**Adjusting Oven Temperature:** If you find the oven temperature inconsistent, you can manually adjust it. Navigate to USER PREFERENCES and set the desired adjustment.

**Temperature Display Selection:** You can set the temperature display to Fahrenheit or Celsius through the "USER PREF" section.

**Audio Control:** The audio settings can be adjusted to minimize sounds during operation. Use the "USER PREF" to toggle sound options.

**Re-setting to Default Factory Settings:** To return all settings to factory defaults, press "USER PREF" and navigate to the reset options.

### MY FAVORITES 

This feature allows you to save cooking preferences for easy access. Store up to three cooking settings in memory. To save a favorite setting, navigate to the "OVEN" section during operation and press "MY FAVORITE." Recalling and saving favorites is straightforward—simply press the corresponding buttons for desired adjustments.

### SABBATH FEATURE (FOR JEWISH SABBATH HOLIDAYS)

Configure the oven to comply with Sabbath requirements by ensuring the bake function operates continuously without interruptions. Press to activate the "BAKE" setting, adjusting temperatures as needed. IMPORTANT: Other functions besides "BAKE" are disabled once activated. Follow the appropriate steps to ensure the temperature is set correctly and the cooking process is initiated.

SABBATH FEATURE (CONTINUED)
To initiate the Sabbath feature, press BAKE and set the temperature to 425°F, then press START twice. To disable the feature, press CANCEL. To switch off the oven during this process, hold both HI and LO for at least a few seconds. The oven can then resume functioning automatically, and the Sabbath indicator will display. If necessary, press the Sabbath button again and hold HI and LO to deactivate the feature.

USING THE SELF-CLEAN FEATURE
Allow the oven to cool before starting the cleaning process. Always wear protective gear when manually cleaning. Do not interrupt the self-cleaning cycle or attempt to cancel it while the oven is hot. Avoid using harsh cleaners or coatings inside the self-cleaning compartment. Do not place food that requires high temperatures inside as it can result in fires. Follow these guidelines to effectively use the self-cleaning function and maintain a safe cooking environment.

GENERAL CLEANING
Regularly clean the oven after use to prevent buildup. Remove spills and heavy residues promptly to limit the need for extensive cleaning later. Use a gentle cloth for painted or decorative surfaces and avoid abrasive cleaners that could scratch them. For best results, perform regular maintenance to reduce the amount of deep cleaning required. 

CLEANING HINGE SLOTS
For oven door maintenance, refer to the special instructions related to door removal and reinstallation. Handle with care to avoid scratches and ensure the door functions properly.

OVEN LIGHT
The oven light operates when the door is closed. It must be secured with a protective shield and can be cleaned carefully when the oven is cool. Always wear protection when servicing the light to prevent burns.

BROILER PAN CLEANING TIPS
Avoid covering the broiler pan during use. Clean grease promptly to avoid buildup and ensure safe cooking conditions.

COMMON ISSUES
If the oven control beeps or displays an error code, press CANCEL to reset the system. Ensure that the oven is connected properly, and consult an authorized service provider if issues persist. If the oven light does not function, check for a burned-out bulb and replace as needed.

SELF-CLEANING CYCLE ISSUES
If the self-cleaning cycle fails, confirm that the door is closed and follow the operational instructions. If excessive smoke occurs during broiling, reposition the meat to maintain proper clearance and avoid dripping grease.

POOR BAKING RESULTS
Ensure that oven temperature settings are accurate and that the oven isn’t overcrowded, as these factors significantly affect cooking performance.

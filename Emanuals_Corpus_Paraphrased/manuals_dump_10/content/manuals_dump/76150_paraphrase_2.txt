### Safety Instructions
Follow these precautions meticulously. Non-compliance could result in injury or property damage.

#### Warnings:
- **Flammable and Electrical Hazards:** To minimize the risk of fire or electric shock, ensure proper installation in accordance with local regulations, including those from the National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) and the American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers (ASHRAE).
- **Service and Cleaning:** Ensure the unit is powered off and disconnected before any servicing or cleaning.

### Package Verification
Ensure all package contents are accounted for prior to installation. Plan the installation location to allow for access and proper clearances as specified.

### Duct Locations
- **Top Duct Locations:** E30WV60EPS, E36WV60EPS, E308WV60ES, E368WV60ES
- **Rear Duct Locations:** E488WV120S

### Duct Planning
To avoid fire hazards, ducts must be correctly routed within walls and ceilings. Maintain proper sizing and minimize tight bends in ductwork to ensure optimal airflow.

### Duct Preparation
Secure ducts to prevent escape of combustion by-products. Follow recommended straight duct lengths for improved performance.

### Cabinet Preparation
Allow necessary clearance around the unit to prevent heat damage, especially at the rear and top.

### Installation Requirements
Electrical supply must be grounded and compliant with American National Standards Institute (ANSI) and NFPA codes. Ensure installation meets all safety protocols.

### Installing the Hood
Mount the hood using the provided temporary board for initial alignment before securing it in place.

### Electrical Connections
Ensure the power supply is disconnected before making any connections. Verify that the electrical supply matches the specifications stated in the manual.

### Verifying Operation
After installation, turn on the power to test the functionality of lights and blower. Confirm all features are operating as intended before finalizing the setup.

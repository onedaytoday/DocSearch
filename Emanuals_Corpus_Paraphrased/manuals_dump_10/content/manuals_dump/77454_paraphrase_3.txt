**WARNING:** To prevent fire or electric shock, avoid exposing the unit to rain or moisture.

### Preparation for Use

**Power Sources:** This CD player operates on AA batteries or an optional AC adapter (included). Using any other power source may void your warranty.

**Installing the Batteries:** Open the battery compartment and insert AA batteries according to the polarity indicated. Ensure the cover is properly secured afterward.

**When to Change Batteries:** Replace the batteries when the battery indicator appears on the display or when you notice reduced performance.

**Using an Optional AC/DC Adapter:** If using an optional AC adapter, it should be 4.5V DC, negative tip. Connect it to the player and plug it into a standard AC outlet.

**Battery Precautions:** Always use the specified battery type and observe the correct polarity to prevent damage. Do not mix old and new batteries or recharge non-rechargeable batteries.

### Listening to the Radio

To switch to the radio, set the CD/RADIO switch to RADIO and turn the power on by pressing the POWER ON PLAY/PAUSE button. To select AM or FM, use the ASP/BAND button. Use the TUN. UP or TUN. DOWN buttons to navigate between stations. Pressing these buttons repeatedly will display the station frequency; release them once the desired station is found. Adjust the volume as needed. If the ((ST)) indicator appears while listening to FM, check your FM reception and switch to MODE if necessary to improve sound quality. To stop listening, press the STOP button followed by POWER OFF.

### Presetting Stations in Tuner Memory

To save AM and FM stations in memory for easy access, first select the FM band. Press the PROG/MEMORY button once; the memory indicator will activate and flash. To store stations in memory, press the PROG/MEMORY button again, which will flash, allowing you to select a station. Repeat this process for other stations using the ASP/BAND button to switch bands. Note that after pressing the PROG/MEMORY button, it will remain flashing for a few seconds to confirm storage. If unsuccessful, you may need to reattempt the process for the desired station.

### Recall Preset Stations

To access preset stations, turn the unit on and use the ASP/BAND button to choose the desired band. Use the PRESET UP or PRESET DOWN buttons to cycle through preset stations. You can also go directly to a particular preset station by selecting the M5+ option.

### Changing Preset Stations

To change a preset station, press the PROG/MEMORY button followed by the PRESET UP or PRESET DOWN buttons. The memory indicator will flash to indicate a shift in presets. Archived presets can be overwritten with new selections.

### Antenna Information

For FM, using headphones can enhance reception. If AM reception is poor, try rotating the antenna slightly to improve the signal.

### Care and Maintenance

To clean the cabinet, use a soft cloth. If necessary, dampen the cloth with a mild cleaning solution, then wipe the surface. For lens cleaning, first remove any dust with a brush or blower, then gently wipe it with a microfiber cloth. To avoid damaging the lens, refrain from using any harsh cleaners.

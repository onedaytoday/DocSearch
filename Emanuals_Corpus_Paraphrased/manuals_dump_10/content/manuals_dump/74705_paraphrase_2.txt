# ERN Instruction Manual

### IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION
This instruction manual should be kept for reference. If the appliance is sold or transferred to another owner, it’s essential that they receive this manual to understand how to operate it safely. This information is crucial for safe usage. Because this appliance is heavy, be cautious when moving it. Always disconnect it from the power supply when performing maintenance or servicing. Avoid exposing the appliance to heat sources or water. The back of the appliance should be positioned as specified in the instructions. Allow the appliance to cool down completely after use. Keep it away from direct sunlight and refrain from placing items like food directly from the freezer without proper precautions, as this may lead to frostbite or freezer burn. Follow manufacturer guidelines meticulously.

### OPERATION
Before using the appliance, ensure you read the instructions thoroughly. Set the thermostat according to the desired temperature. Factors affecting cooling performance may include ambient temperature, food load, and how often the door is opened. Maintaining the temperature below 16°C is crucial for optimal operation. If defrosting, switch off the thermostat as indicated.

To preserve food quality, ensure cooked meals are never refrozen. Refrigerate fresh food promptly and follow specific guidelines for thawing and cooking various food types to minimize health risks. Use suitable containers for storage to maintain food integrity.

### MAINTENANCE AND CLEANING
Before cleaning, disconnect the appliance from the electricity supply. Use suitable cleaning agents—avoid detergents, abrasive powders, or highly perfumed products as these may damage the interior surfaces. To replace the light, ensure the appliance is unplugged and follow the provided instructions carefully.

### DEFROSTING
Regularly defrost the appliance when ice buildup exceeds the specified thickness. Switch off the thermostat prior to starting the defrosting process and follow guidelines for managing the thawed water effectively.

### TROUBLESHOOTING
If issues arise, refer to the troubleshooting section of this manual. Common symptoms include excessive frost or temperature fluctuations. Ensure the thermostat is set correctly and that the door seals well. If problems persist, contact Service Force or an authorized technician for assistance.

### CUSTOMER SERVICE AND SPARE PARTS
For service or replacement parts, contact Service Force. For further information, visit the website or reach out to Customer Care directly with your inquiries.

### INSTALLATION INSTRUCTIONS

**Warning:** If disposing of an old appliance that has a locking mechanism on the door, ensure it is rendered unusable to prevent children from becoming trapped inside.

**Positioning:** The appliance should be placed in a location that meets specified temperature ratings. Optimal operating conditions are:
- SN: +10°C to +32°C
- ST: +18°C to +38°C
- T: +18°C to +43°C

Avoid placing the appliance in areas exposed to sunlight or near heat sources. Ensure proper clearance and follow installation instructions closely.

**ELECTRICAL CONNECTIONS:**
WARNING: This appliance must be grounded. Ensure correct installation as follows:
- Wires are color-coded as follows: Earth (green/yellow), Neutral (blue), Live (brown). 
- If not equipped, use a plug that conforms to BSI standards.

### INSTALLATION PROCESS

Follow the steps below for installing the appliance:

1. Unscrew and remove any necessary panels.
2. Adjust the recess height to achieve the required clearances.
3. Align the appliance according to the installation guide, ensuring it fits securely.
4. Use screws provided to secure all covers and components.

Lastly, ensure proper wiring to maintain optimal functionality of the appliance. If adjustments are needed, follow the guidance for necessary clearances.

### Installation Instructions for Freestanding Electric Range

**Important: Installation and service must be conducted by a qualified installer. Retain these instructions for local electrical inspector use and future reference.**

### Location and Clearance Requirements
Verify the installation site and electrical supply configurations as outlined. Ensure the following minimum clearances are maintained:
- **Top of cooking surface to unprotected wood or metal cabinet:** Minimum clearance required.
- **If cabinets are protected by at least flame-retardant millboard:** Identify the required clearance.

Adhering to the specified dimensions will help prevent property damage, fire hazards, and improper cabinet or countertop cuts. To avoid burns or fire risks from reaching over heated surfaces, avoid placing cabinet storage directly above cooktop. If storage above is necessary, reduce risks by installing a range hood that extends at least horizontally beyond the bottom of the cabinets.

### Safety Instructions
Failure to comply with these instructions may result in fire, electrical shock, or other serious hazards. Ensure all electrical connections adhere to local codes and ordinances. These instructions should remain accessible for the consumer's reference during operation.

When using heat, ensure the appliance is properly grounded to prevent electrical hazards. The coverings around the unit should withstand heat exposure.

### Installation Procedure
#### Pre-Installation Requirements
Gather necessary tools, including wrenches and screwdrivers. Refer to the normal installation steps and instructions for the anti-tip bracket—this is crucial for safety.

**Anti-Tip Bracket Installation:**
To minimize the risk of tipping, install the provided bracket securely. Follow the instructions to attach the bracket correctly to the wall or floor. Ensure that the screws are inserted completely.

### Electrical Connection Requirements
The appliance must be properly grounded, complying with the National ANSI/NFPA standards. Ensure that power supply cords are properly rated and connected to prevent pinch points. The installation may involve connecting a supply cord with three or four conductors.

#### Power Cord Connections
- **4A:** Locate the rear access cover, secure it, and follow the instructions provided to connect the power cord correctly.
- **Important Note:** Do not loosen factory-installed ground screws; maintain connections as illustrated in the instructions.

### Final Steps
Carefully slide the range into its permanent location, ensuring proper clearances and engaging the anti-tip bracket fully to secure the appliance. 

By adhering to these detailed installation instructions, you can ensure the safety and functionality of your freestanding electric range.

### INSTRUCCIONES DE INSTALACIÓN PARA LA ESTUFA ELÉCTRICA

**Advertencia:** El uso de un juego de cables de alimentación de calibre inadecuado puede provocar riesgos de incendio o choque eléctrico. Es fundamental seguir las instrucciones de instalación y utilizar el soporte del sujetacables.

#### Conexión Eléctrica a la Estufa

1. **Conexión de los Bornes:**
   - Note que las conexiones deben hacerse en la parte trasera de la estufa.
   - Asegúrese de conectar correctamente los terminales (no los afloje).

#### Especificaciones Importantes:

- **Modelos Compatibles:**
  - (14-50R)
  - (10-50R)
  
- **Conexiones del Cable de Alimentación:**
  - Conectar el cable negro al borne correspondiente.
  - El cable blanco (neutro) también debe ser conectado adecuadamente.
  - El cable rojo se debe conectar al borne correspondiente.

#### Notas Adicionales:

- **Deslizamiento de la Estufa:**
  - Deslizar la estufa con cuidado hasta su ubicación final, asegurándose de que esté correctamente enganchada al soporte antivuelco.
  
- **Conexiones de Tierra:**
  - Asegúrese de que el tornillo de conexión a tierra esté apretado.
  - Mantenga todos los cables terminales firmemente conectados a la placa de conexión a tierra.

Recuerde que las conexiones deben ser permanentes y todos los bornes deben quedarse ajustados para garantizar un funcionamiento seguro y efectivo de la estufa.

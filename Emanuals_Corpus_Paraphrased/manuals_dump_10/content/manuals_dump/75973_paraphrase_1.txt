### **Important Safety Instructions**
For optimal use and safety of the appliance, refer to the instructions provided. It is crucial that heavy objects are handled with care to avoid injury. The appliance should be correctly positioned according to the specified guidelines. Only qualified technicians should carry out servicing to adhere to safety standards. Child safety measures should be observed to prevent suffocation or accidents. Avoid exposing the appliance to heat sources and ensure adequate ventilation behind the unit. 

### **Description of the Appliance**
The refrigerator features adjustable shelves and operates with a thermostat. To adjust settings, turn the thermostat to the desired temperature. Food storage performance can vary, so plan accordingly, especially in extreme weather conditions.

### **Food Storage Guidelines**
To maintain freshness:
- Cover food with protective materials such as polythene or plastic wrap.
- Organize food items by type to maximize cooling efficiency.
- Ensure that all bottles and containers are properly sealed.

### **Normal Operating Sounds**
You may hear various sounds while the refrigerator operates. These include a consistent whirring from the compressor and occasional clicks from the thermostat. 

### **Health and Safety Guidelines**
To ensure food safety:
- Monitor the internal temperature to stay within the recommended range (from 10°C to 32°C).
- Regularly clean compartments and ensure no food is left uncovered to prevent contamination.
- Follow procedures for cooling cooked foods before refrigeration.

### **Defrosting**
Regularly defrost the appliance to prevent ice build-up. The defrost function activates automatically in its designated area.

### **Maintenance and Cleaning**
Always disconnect the refrigerator from the power supply before cleaning. Avoid using harsh detergents or abrasive materials inside, as they can damage surfaces. Ensure drainage channels are clear for proper water flow.

### **Light Replacement**
If the interior light fails, switch off the power, remove the cover, and replace the bulb as instructed. 

### **Troubleshooting**
If the refrigerator is not functioning correctly:
- Check the power supply and connections.
- Ensure that the thermostat is set appropriately.
- Inspect for blockages that may hinder operation.

### **Service and Spare Parts**
For servicing or purchasing parts, contact Service Force. Have your purchase details ready when calling for assistance.

**Installation Guidelines:**
Positioning should ensure efficiency and must be maintained in a location with ambient temperatures between below 10°C (50°F) and above 32°C (90°F) to avoid overheating from direct sunlight. Adequate space should be left for airflow around the unit, particularly at the back and top. Ensure that any obstructions at both the back and the top are removed.

**Assembly Instructions for Unit**:
To disassemble, ensure careful handling by following these steps:
1. Gently tilt the unit back while allowing clearance above (approximately 6 inches).
2. Unscrew the top hinge and detach the top component.
3. Remove the legs and carefully unscrew any remaining screws from the base.

**Electrical Connection:**
**WARNING:** This appliance must be properly earthed. It is essential to use wiring that conforms to safety standards. Disconnect the appliance from the power supply before performing any maintenance.

- The colored wires should be connected according to the following color coding:
  - **Earth (Green/Yellow)**: Connect to the earth terminal.
  - **Neutral (Blue)**: Connect to the neutral terminal.
  - **Live (Brown)**: Connect to the live terminal.

Use a fused connection meeting BS1362 standards. Ensure compliance with E.E.C. directives to avoid interference from electrical breakdowns.

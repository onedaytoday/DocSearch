## OPERATION

To use the appliance effectively, set and adjust the thermostat carefully. External factors such as prolonged high temperatures can affect cooling efficiency, so adjust the thermostat to maintain optimal conditions based on the food being stored. 

When fresh food is added, allow the appliance to stabilize for several hours. If food begins to thaw, remember that defrosted cooked food should not be refrozen. For best results, always cook and consume thawed products promptly.

## HINTS AND TIPS

Keep food well-organized and ensure that air can circulate freely around items. Avoid overpacking, as this can obstruct airflow and reduce efficiency. Use protective covers for food items to maintain freshness.

## MAINTENANCE AND CLEANING

Before cleaning, disconnect the appliance from the power supply. Use a damp cloth and mild cleaning agents; avoid harsh chemicals, abrasives, or wax products as these can damage surfaces.

For bulb replacements, switch off the appliance. Unscrew the light cover carefully to access the bulb, replacing as necessary.

### Installation Instructions

**Warning**: If you are disposing of an old appliance with a locking mechanism, ensure it is rendered unusable to prevent accidental entrapment of children.

**Positioning**: Place the appliance in a location that meets the indicated temperature ratings:
- SN: +10°C to +32°C
- N: +16°C to +32°C
- ST: +18°C to +38°C
- T: +18°C to +43°C

Avoid direct sunlight and sources of heat. If the surrounding temperature is too high, the appliance may not function correctly.

**Wiring**: This appliance must be properly grounded. Ensure that the electrical supply is fitted by a qualified technician. The wires should be color-coded as follows:
- **Earth**: Green/Yellow
- **Neutral**: Blue
- **Live**: Brown

Use plugs that comply with the BSI362 ASTA or BSI standards. Connect the wires to the correct terminals as specified.
